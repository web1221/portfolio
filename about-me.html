About me

I was born and raised in a suburb outside of Washington DC, called Silver Spring, Maryland. I dropped out of high school at the age of 16, and applied to my local community college. I attended the community college for a couple years where I studied criminal justice. The deeper I went into the courses, the more I realized this path wasn't for me. I've worked a few different jobs; I've delivered pizza for dominos, I've worked for my family's cleaning business, and in 2018 I moved to Portland, OR and found a job making ribbon racks for the military. I started getting interested in programming in early 2019. I've had several family members who have worked with, or around, computer programming and coding. It really seems like a career that can fuel my passion for problem solving, and is also going in the right direction with the times. I started trying to self-teach a bit, but with a full 40-hour-plus work week it was hard to find the motivation needed to make strides self-taught. I would feel like I was progressing, and then would hit a wall that seemed to throw me back to the beginning. That's when I started looking at coding-specific schools. I was looking for something that would push me to code while getting human-to-human contact. Epicodus seem to have everything I was looking for, along with a part-time program that would really help me gauge my interest in coding. So far Epicodus been an amazing tool, and has really helped me with concepts I didn't even know I was missing!
